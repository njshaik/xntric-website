Jewelry

Why I Started
Making Earrings

Shujauddin5 Dec 20243 min read

It started with a gift problem. I wanted to give my sister something for Eid that felt considered — not something off a shelf, not something generic. I'd been making larger pieces for a while: stands, trays, small boxes. But jewellery had never crossed my mind, because I'd always thought of it as a different discipline entirely.

I was wrong. It's the same discipline, just smaller. And smaller turns out to be harder in all the most interesting ways.

"When the piece fits in your palm, there's nowhere for a mistake to hide."

The Scale Problem

When I first tried cutting earring blanks from a sheet of walnut, I broke four pieces before I got one clean. The wood was fine — the problem was my sawing technique. At small scales, any wobble in the cut becomes visible. I had to slow down considerably, let the saw do the work, and stop gripping the wood as if it might escape.

Blanks before shaping (left) · Finished pair on a dark background (right)

What Wood Works

Not all wood is suitable for earrings. It needs to be dense enough to hold fine detail without chipping, light enough to wear comfortably all day, and stable enough not to warp in humidity. Walnut is my go-to — it hits all three. I've also had good results with cherry and, surprisingly, coconut shell composite.

Walnut · Cherry · Detail carving in progress

The gift landed well. My sister wore the earrings to three consecutive occasions, which I consider the highest possible endorsement. I've been making them ever since — and now they're the most requested piece in the workshop.
